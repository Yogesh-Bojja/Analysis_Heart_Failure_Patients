1.Introduction: ¶

Every year around 17 million people die due to Cardiovascular diseases. This mainly consists of heart failures. There are various laboratory tests available through which a person can obtain some useful results. For example Creatinine phosphokinase responsible in regeneration of ATP, when in excess can lead to numerous heart related diseases. In the same way there are other components present in the body which can guide us to keep a check on the progression of heart failure. Results of these different tests and some daily habits and some other features can be used in Machine Learning to predict the survival rate of a person from Cardiovascular disease which can be unseen by a doctor.

1.1.Dataset Source: ¶

Chicco, D., 2020. UCI Machine Learning Repository: Heart failure clinical records Data Set. [online] Archive.ics.uci.edu. Available at: https://archive.ics.uci.edu/ml/datasets/Heart+failure+clinical+records [Accessed 10 April 2021].

1.2.Dataset Details: ¶

This dataset consists of records of heart failure patients. It has 299 records and 13 attributes in it. It is a multivariate dataset. Different clinical features are provided in this dataset. There are 105 women and 194 men in this dataset ranging between age group of 40 and 95.

1.3.Dataset Features: ¶

Feature Name Data type Units Description
Age Integer years Patient age
Anaemia Integer boolean Reduced blood cells or hemoglobon(0: No Anaemia and 1: Anaemia)
High Blood Pressure Integer boolean Hypertension in patient(0: No High Blood Pressure and 1: High Blood Pressure
Creatinine Phosphokinase (CPK) Integer mcg/L CPK enzyme level in blood
Diabetes Integer boolean Diabetes of patient(0: No Diabetes and 1: Diabetes
Ejection Fraction Integer mcg/L CPK enzyme level in blood
Platelets Integer kiloplatelets/L Blood Platelets count
Sex Integer boolean Gender of a patient(0: Women and 1: Men)
Serum Ceratinine Real mg/dL Serum Ceratinine level in blood
Serum Sodium Integer mEq/L Serum Sodium level in blood
Smoking Integer boolean Does the patient smoke(0: Not Smoking and 1: Smoking)
Time Integer days Follow up period
Death Event Integer boolean Did the patient die in the follow up period(0: No and 1: Yes)

1.4.Target Feature: ¶

The Death Event attribute is the target variable in our dataset. We will use this target variable to predict the number of survived patients based upon the clinical test results provided of the given above attributes. We will make our model based on the attributes that gives us the best result.

2.Goal & Objectives: ¶

Objective of this assessment is to preprocess the data collected from the survey and infer valuable insights from it. After preprocessing and cleaning our data we will be visualizing and exploring the data to gain fruitful correlations between different features with the target variable.

3.Data Cleaning &Preprocessing: ¶

In our dataset we have performed the necessary data cleaning and preprocessing steps. We have checked for the missing values and null values in our dataset. We have also checked for negative values in age attribute and even for some outliers in it. There is no column with constant data, redundant data or informative data in the given dataset hence there is no need of discarding any of the column from our table. So according to our analysis there is no data cleaning and Preprocessing required in the given dataset.

5.Summary & Conclusions:¶

From the survey conducted of 299 people suffering from heart failure it is concusive that age of a person ,blood pressure , ejection fraction , creatinine phosphokinase are directly related and are major factors leading to heart failure and death of the patient .While habit of smoking , gender of the person and platelet count are not considered crucial in developing heart failure .People suffering from diabetes have same serum sodium and are at similar risk like the non-diabetics.
